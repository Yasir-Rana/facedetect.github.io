The use of facial recognition technology to find missing persons is a growing trend in law enforcement. The technology can help speed up the process of identifying people who have gone missing by quickly comparing the image of the missing person to a database of known individuals. However, there are concerns about the accuracy and privacy implications of using facial recognition technology, particularly in the absence of clear regulations. Some people worry that the technology could lead to false identifications, which could have serious consequences for both the person being wrongly accused and their families. Additionally, there is concern that the use of facial recognition technology could infringe on people's privacy and civil liberties.

“ Face search refers to the technology used to search for a matching image of a person by using facial recognition algorithms. This technology can be used in a variety of applications, including security, social media, and retail, to name a few. In a face search, an image of a person is compared with a database of images to find a matching face. The accuracy of face search technology depends on factors such as the quality of the image being searched and the size of the database being searched against.”
